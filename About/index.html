About

My name is Di Pan (潘迪). I am a Master's student in Physics at the École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), working in the Fields & Strings Laboratory under the supervision of Prof. João Penedones. Before EPFL, I completed my Bachelor's degree in Physics at Tsinghua University, where I worked under the supervision of Prof. Wei Song.

I am broadly interested in the deep structural questions of quantum gravity: how does spacetime geometry emerge from quantum degrees of freedom? What is the microscopic origin of black hole entropy? My work approaches these questions through the lenses of holography, conformal field theory, and lattice field theory.

Outside physics, I maintain a personal blog (The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya) where I write about code, language, and small things noticed in the rain.

Research Interests

  • Holography and AdS/CFT
  • Black hole thermodynamics and the gravitational path integral
  • Lattice field theory and Villain constructions
  • Generalized and non-invertible symmetries
  • Quantum information in quantum gravity
