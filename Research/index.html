Research

My research sits at the intersection of quantum gravity, holography, and lattice field theory. Below are my current and recent projects.

Ongoing Projects

Modified Villain Construction with Boundaries

with Prof. João Penedones (EPFL)  ·  2024–present

We develop lattice Villain Hamiltonians with boundary conditions as a systematic discretization scheme for quantum field theories. The Villain formulation avoids the usual problems with compact scalars on the lattice by extending the field configuration space. Current results include matching the anomaly-free condition in the continuum limit and reproducing the Reflection Matrix for a bosonized lattice model. The project targets a preprint in 2026.

Zero Mode Corrections to the Kerr Black Hole Partition Function

with Prof. Wei Song (Tsinghua University)  ·  2023–present

We investigate quantum corrections arising from zero modes in the gravitational path integral computation of the Kerr black hole partition function. Zero modes must be treated separately in the one-loop determinant and contribute non-trivially to the density of states. This project connects to the microscopic counting of extremal Kerr microstates and the Kerr/CFT correspondence.

Background

Before these projects, I developed familiarity with: Euclidean quantum gravity and the gravitational path integral; 2d CFT and the Virasoro algebra; entanglement entropy and the Ryu–Takayanagi formula; JT gravity and its matrix-model dual; and the near-horizon geometry of extremal black holes.
